World Health Organization (WHO)

Agenda: Sexual & Reproductive Rights of Women with special emphasis on Abortion and Child Birth

WHO is a specialized agency of the UN that is concerned with public health. Its primary goal is to direct international health within the UN’s system. Reproductive rights are legal rights & concern the freedom relating to reproduction & reproductive health which vary amongst different countries of the world.

Human Rights Council (HRC)

Agenda: Protection & Promotion of Human Rights in Post-Conflict Societies with special emphasis on Middle East and Africa

The Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body within the United Nations, strengthening promotion and protection of human rights and addressing situations of human rights violation, around the globe. The agenda on the floor would be to discuss reconstruction & consolidation of peace & security & attainment of sustainable socio-economic development in a war shattered country.

Loksabha

Agenda: Article 370: Special Autonomous status to the state of Jammu & Kashmir

The lower house of the Parliament of India, Lok Sabha consists of representatives elected directly by the people of India. The seat allotment is done on various factors such that every state gets a fair representation. Article 370 of Indian Constitution is the article that grants special autonomous status to the state of Jammu & Kashmir. The article is drafted in Part XXI of the constitution: Temporary, Transitional & Special Provisions.

Indo Pak Partition Committee 1947

Agenda: Discussing the nature and future of the two successive dominions of British Crown.

The Partition Committee was formed for the purpose of transferring and deciding the nature of the two successive dominions of British Crown, India and Pakistan. It was chaired by Lord Mountbatten and its members were the political leaders of British India, representing the Indian National Congress and the All India Muslim League.
